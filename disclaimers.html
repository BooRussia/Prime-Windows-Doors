Performance Claims & Substantiation

Numerical performance statistics displayed on this website — including figures related to energy savings, noise reduction, heat gain reduction, homeowner insurance savings, and UV blocking — represent maximum potential results derived from manufacturer-published specifications and independent third-party laboratory testing under controlled conditions. These figures are not guarantees of results for any individual installation.

Actual performance varies significantly based on: product specification and configuration selected; home construction type, age, and existing insulation values; building orientation and sun exposure; local climate conditions; quality of installation; post-installation maintenance; and other factors outside Prime Windows & Doors’ control.

Impact Glass — “4× Stronger” Claim

The comparative claim that impact-rated laminated glass is approximately 4× stronger than standard double-pane glass (marked † on this site) is based on structural load, deflection resistance, and breakage resistance testing of laminated impact glass versus standard double-pane insulating glass units (IGUs). This comparison references the following applicable standards:

Referenced Standards

ASTM E1886 — Standard Test Method for Performance of Exterior Windows, Curtain Walls, Doors, and Impact Protective Systems Impacted by Missile(s) and Exposed to Cyclic Pressure Differentials.

ASTM E1996 — Standard Specification for Performance of Exterior Windows, Curtain Walls, Doors, and Impact Protective Systems Hit by Windborne Debris in Hurricanes.

Florida Building Code, Chapter 14 / Section 1609 — Wind Loads and Impact Resistance Requirements, as administered by the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR).

Miami-Dade NOA (Notice of Acceptance) — Impact product certifications may also reference Miami-Dade product approval testing where applicable.

The “4×” figure reflects comparative structural performance under specific test conditions and does not apply uniformly to all impact product lines, glass thicknesses, or installation configurations. Consult product-specific NOA documentation and your sales representative for the exact performance ratings of any product under consideration.

Performance Bar Figures

  • Energy savings figures reflect manufacturer-stated maximum reductions in heating/cooling load for qualifying Energy Star®-rated products versus single-pane baselines unless otherwise specified.
  • Insurance savings figures reflect ranges reported by select Florida insurance carriers for impact-rated product upgrades; actual savings depend on carrier, policy, home age, and location.
  • Noise reduction figures reflect laboratory STC (Sound Transmission Class) ratings for qualifying laminated glass products versus standard IGUs.
  • UV block figures (up to 99%) reflect published performance of LoE® glass coatings for qualifying product specifications.
  • Heat gain reduction figures reflect Solar Heat Gain Coefficient (SHGC) improvement data for qualifying low-emissivity products.

All performance figures are presented in good faith based on available manufacturer data. Prime Windows & Doors makes no independent warranty as to the accuracy of manufacturer-stated performance figures.
